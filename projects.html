Snake AI Gamebot

As the final project for my end-of-year physics class, I decided to play around with using AI to play games. The first game I did was SNAKE, where the training data was based on my own playing. The data contained information such as its direction, the distance to the wall, and the vector from the head to the red dot. I then trained the model against this data.

"The Clarkanoid"

You are a University professor, yet suddenly you have turned into a camel. Your friend, the owl, has been taken by the Imperials, which the all-powerful "Ark" controls. Recover the owl by going on an adventure through six terrains, collecting valuable items, defeating a dragon in space, and finally battling the Ark.

This game was made with two partners over the course of two months. The entire game is coded in OCaml (though it uses an SDL library for graphics). The game takes advantage of both functional and imperative features of OCaml. The Clarkanoid implements its own Entity-Component-System and features entirely custom pixel art, unique AI's for each enemy, and custom soundtracks, animations, and storylines.

Traveling Salesman Problem

As one of my high school projects, I decided to look into the traveling salesman problem. Written in Java and using Swing, I made a program to help visualize the path finding. The user places specific points on the GUI, and then chooses the algorithm to find the shortest cycle to the starting position.

The algorithms feature brute force, a greedy algorithm, a greedy algorithm with swapping, and an ant-colony heuristic, in which several "ants" travel along the paths and leave "pheremones". The paths with the most pheremones tends to suggest the quickest path.
